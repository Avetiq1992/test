Plase in Armenia
Kaskad

The Cascade, located behind the Opera and Ballet Theatre, between Mesrop Mashtots Avenue and Baghramyan and Moskovyan streets, originates in a small square where a monument to the architect Tamanyan was erected, thinking about the main plan in his life. The purpose of building a pompous staircase made of milky tuff was to connect the lower and upper cities, located high in the mountains.

Erebuni fortress

In 782 BC. e. Argishti I, the king of the Kingdom of Van, decided to create the Erebuni fortress on the Red Hill as a strategic object to secure the Urartians in the Ararat Valley, leaving the date of the founding of the city on a stone stele

Noravanq

Noravank monastery complex is the pearl of Armenian religious architecture. Located 120 km from Yerevan, it attracts tourists with churches of the 13th century against the backdrop of unearthly landscapes - sheer red rocks of the Arpa River Gorge in the village of Jermuk.

Zvartnoc

At the beginning of the 20th century, 10 km from Yerevan, a group of researchers discovered the ruins of an ancient temple, buried up to the very roof under a layer of earth and sand. After excavating the building and reconstructing its original appearance, scientists came to the conclusion that one of the most beautiful and large-scale religious buildings of early medieval Armenia, the Temple of Zvartnots, also known as the “Temple of Vigilant Angels”, was located on the site of the hill.

Sanahin

The monastic complex of Sanahin, spread out in the delta of the river. Debed is considered one of the most inaccessible religious buildings on the territory of Armenia: it can only be reached on foot, having previously overcome many kilometers from Yerevan.
